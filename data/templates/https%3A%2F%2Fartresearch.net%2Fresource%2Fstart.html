Thanks to ongoing work on record reconciliation across photo archives, you can compare images, bibliographic resources, attributions, and reconstruct the histories of artworks and photographs in one place.

Compare scholarly images and data from different sources

Portrait of a young lady, Pollaiolo (c. 1465)

Studying different reproductions of Pollaiolo’s Portrait of a young lady from multiple photo archives allows researchers to compare variations in inscriptions, attributions, provenance, mounts, printing techniques and framing, thus giving a more complete research history of the painting.

How we use state-of-the-art technologies

Our digitized photographic collections go on a long journey to reach artresearch.net. We explore state-ofthe- art technologies, like computer vision and linked open data, to enrich records with data from multiple sources, reconcile artworks, people, and places, and share data with the scholarly community in interoperable formats according to the CIDOC-CRM ontology.
